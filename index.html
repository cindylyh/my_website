About Me

Hello! My name is Yi Ham Lin (Cindy), and I am currently a third-year student at Northeastern University majoring in Data Science and Business Administration with a concentration in FinTech. I am originally from Hong Kong and I am particularly interested in the intersection of data analysis, business operations, and finance. I enjoy learning how data can help organizations make better decisions and understand complex patterns in business and financial markets.

Last semester, I completed my co-op in Hong Kong as an Equity Research Intern. During this experience, I had the opportunity to analyze companies, review financial data, and support research on investment opportunities. This experience strengthened my interest in financial markets and showed me how analytical skills can be applied to real-world investment decisions. I also gained valuable experience working with financial reports, industry analysis, and market data, which helped me better understand how investment professionals evaluate companies and identify potential opportunities. Overall, the co-op allowed me to connect what I learned in the classroom with practical applications in the financial industry.

Outside of academics, I believe it is important to explore interests beyond the classroom. Two hobbies that I especially enjoy are traveling and snowboarding. Traveling allows me to experience different cultures, landscapes, and perspectives around the world. For example, I visited several cities in Italy last December and was fascinated by the contrast between northern cities such as Milan and Florence and southern areas like Naples and Sicily. Each place had its own unique atmosphere and culture.

Snowboarding is another activity that I enjoy because it combines adventure, challenge, and the beauty of nature. Riding down a mountain surrounded by snow and fresh air is both exciting and relaxing. These hobbies allow me to stay active, explore new environments, and maintain a balance between academic work and personal interests.
